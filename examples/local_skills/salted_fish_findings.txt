Key details from the facts:
- Salted fish is fish cured with dry salt and preserved for later eating
- It's a method of preserving fish using dry edible salt
- The salting process inhibits microorganism growth by drawing water out of microbial cells through osmosis
- It was the only widely available method of preserving fish until the 19th century
- Salted fish (along with dried fish) is a staple in many diets around the world including regions like Scandinavia, West Africa, South Asia, Southeast Asia, and parts of Canada and Russia
- Like other salt-cured meats, it provides preserved animal protein even without refrigeration
