Based only on the provided facts, D-Wave Systems is:

A quantum computing company that claims to be the world's first company to sell computers that exploit quantum effects in their operation. D-Wave specializes in quantum annealing rather than universal quantum computing, and its computers are designed to solve optimization problems. The company was founded in 1999 by Haig Farris, Geordie Rose, Bob Wiens, and Alexandre Zagoskin, and is headquartered in Palo Alto, California with additional locations in Burnaby, British Columbia and Vienna, California. D-Wave's early customers include Lockheed Martin, the University of Southern California, Google/NASA, and Los Alamos National Laboratory. The company has released several quantum computer systems including the D-Wave One (128-qubit), D-Wave Two (512-qubit), D-Wave 2X (1000+ qubit), D-Wave 2000Q, Advantage, and Advantage2 systems.
