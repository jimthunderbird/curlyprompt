Sugar is a class of sweet-tasting, soluble carbohydrates. Simple sugars (monosaccharides) include glucose, fructose, and galactose. Compound sugars (disaccharides) are made of two bonded monosaccharides, with common examples being sucrose (glucose + fructose), lactose (glucose + galactose), and maltose (two molecules of glucose). White sugar is almost pure sucrose. During digestion, compound sugars are hydrolysed into simple sugars. Longer chains of saccharides are not regarded as sugars and are called oligosaccharides or polysaccharides. Salt is a mineral composed primarily of sodium chloride (NaCl). When used in food, especially in granulated form, it is more formally called table salt. In its natural crystalline mineral form, salt is also known as rock salt or halite.

Salt is essential for life, being the source of essential dietary minerals sodium and chlorine. Saltiness is one of the basic human tastes. It is one of the oldest and most ubiquitous food seasonings, known to uniformly improve taste perception of food. Salt is used in food preservation through methods like salting, brining, and pickling.

Salt has been processed since around 6000 BC, with early evidence from Romania and China. It became an important article of trade, transported across seas, along salt roads, and across the Sahara on camel caravans. Salt was so valuable that nations have gone to war over it and used it to raise tax revenues.

Salt is processed from salt mines and by evaporation of seawater or mineral-rich spring water. The greatest single use for salt is as a feedstock for producing chemicals like caustic soda and chlorine, and for manufacturing products such as plastics and paper pulp. Only a small percentage of global production is used for human consumption.

Salt is essential for human health via its role as an electrolyte and osmotic solute, but excessive consumption increases cardiovascular disease risk including hypertension. The World Health Organization recommends adults consume less than 2,000 mg of sodium (5 grams of salt) per day.
