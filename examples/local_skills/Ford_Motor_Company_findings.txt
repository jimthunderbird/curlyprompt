Based on the provided facts, Ford Motor Company is:

An American multinational automobile manufacturer headquartered in Dearborn, Michigan. It was founded by Henry Ford and incorporated on June 16, 1903. The company sells automobiles and commercial vehicles under the Ford brand, and luxury cars under its Lincoln brand. 

Key characteristics include:
- It is the second-largest American-based automaker and sixth-largest in the world (based on 2022 vehicle production)
- It introduced mass-production methods using moving assembly lines, known as "Fordism"
- The company is controlled by the Ford family through special Class B shares (40% of voting rights)
- It has operations worldwide including the United States, Canada, Mexico, China, India, Europe, and other regions
- Ford is listed on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol F
- The company went through significant financial struggles during the 2008-2010 automotive industry crisis but returned to profitability
- Ford has a history of various brand acquisitions and divestitures (Jaguar, Land Rover, Volvo, Mercury, Edsel, Aston Martin)
- It has been involved in electric vehicle development and has announced plans for significant expansion in EV production
- The company operates joint ventures in several countries including China, Taiwan, Thailand, and Turkey
- Ford Motor Company is also known for its philanthropy through Ford Philanthropy (formerly Ford Motor Company Fund)
