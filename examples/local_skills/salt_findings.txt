Salt is a mineral composed primarily of sodium chloride (NaCl). When used in food, especially in granulated form, it is more formally called table salt. In its natural crystalline mineral form, salt is also known as rock salt or halite.

Salt is essential for life, being the source of essential dietary minerals sodium and chlorine. Saltiness is one of the basic human tastes. It is one of the oldest and most ubiquitous food seasonings, known to uniformly improve taste perception of food. Salting, brining, and pickling are ancient methods of food preservation.

Salt is processed from salt mines and by evaporation of seawater (sea salt) and mineral-rich spring water in shallow pools. The greatest single use for salt is as a feedstock for producing chemicals like caustic soda and chlorine, and in manufacturing products such as plastics and paper pulp. Of the global production of around three hundred million tonnes annually, only a small percentage is used for human consumption.
