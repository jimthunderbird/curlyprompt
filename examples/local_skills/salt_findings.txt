Salt is a mineral composed primarily of sodium chloride (NaCl). When used in food, especially in granulated form, it is more formally called table salt. In its natural crystalline mineral form, salt is also known as rock salt or halite.

Salt is essential for life, being the source of essential dietary minerals sodium and chlorine. Saltiness is one of the basic human tastes. It is one of the oldest and most ubiquitous food seasonings, known to uniformly improve taste perception of food. Salting, brining, and pickling are ancient methods of food preservation.

Salt is processed from salt mines and by evaporation of seawater (sea salt) and mineral-rich spring water in shallow pools. The greatest single use for salt is as a feedstock for producing chemicals like caustic soda and chlorine, and in manufacturing products such as plastics and paper pulp. Of the global production of around three hundred million tonnes annually, only a small percentage is used for human consumption.

- Table salt (granulated form used in food)
- Rock salt/halite (natural crystalline mineral form)
- Sea salt (produced by evaporating seawater)

Salt is essential for life, providing the dietary minerals sodium and chlorine. It is one of the basic human tastes (saltiness) and has been used as a food seasoning, preservative, and flavor enhancer for thousands of years.

Key characteristics:
- Essential for human health as an electrolyte and osmotic solute
- Used in food preservation methods like salting, brining, and pickling
- Processed from salt mines or by evaporating seawater/mineral-rich spring water
- Contains trace elements in different forms (sea salt, rock salt)
- Usually refined for table use with additives like anti-caking agents and iodine

Salt has been crucial to civilization throughout history - it was traded as currency, used in religious ceremonies, and even sparked wars over its control. While necessary for health, excessive consumption increases risk of cardiovascular diseases like hypertension. The World Health Organization recommends limiting intake to less than 2,000 mg of sodium (5 grams of salt) per day.
